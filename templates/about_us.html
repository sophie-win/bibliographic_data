Dr Lee Chu Keong (About Me!)

{# #}

I started graphs.be because I was taken in with the great Argentinian author, Jorge Luis Borges’ quotation, “everything touches everything”. Indeed everything touches everything, and a graph database like Neo4j is the best way of modelling this.

But why offer a Critical Inquiry project on it?

Because I wanted to give students the opportunity to take an actual project from conceptualisation to deployment.

An ideal technical CI project should achieve the following five objectives:

  1. students should learn things about information technology that are completely new to them (i.e., things that have not been taught in the programme)

    2. One of the objectives of the MSc (Knowledge Management) programme is for students to learn how to continue to learn throughout their lifetime. The pace of change and development in information technology is such that the students who aspire to work in this field must be especially adept in being nimble and fast learners.

    In this project, the students have learnt the following languages / frameworks:

    • Python
    • Django
    • Neo4j and Cypher
    • D3 and Neo4jD3

    They also learnt the following, in greater depth:

    • the Font Awesome font and icon toolkit
    • the Bootstrap framework

  2. students should experience the issues and problems faced with deploying the website with an actual host

    3. Making a project work on a local server is very different from making one work on a host. There are lots of settings on both the internet domain registrar, and the hosting service. There are problems to be sorted out with the hosting service. All of which students do not get to experience with when working on a project hosted locally.

    In this project, the students have deployed the entire website in Digital Ocean.

  3. students should be able to give a demonstration of their project when they go for interviews

    4. After completion of the programme, most students will go for job interviews, and during the interviews, they will inevitably be asked about the project they had worked on. Providing a demonstration of the app is the tangible prove the they have worked on it. Unfortunately many student projects lie dormant in GitHub or some other code repository. As a result, they can only talk about their projects during interview, and perhaps show a few screen shots. By deploying it for at least a year, students can show off their projects during their interviews. The hyperlink to their LinkedIn pages in the About Page assures the interviewer that the job applicant has indeed worked on the project, and being able to explore the app in their own time enables the interviewer to see that the project actually works.

  4. students should learn something outside information technology

    5. In the genogram portion of this project, data from the Darwin-Wedgwood family tree was used. Students learn about three eminent members of this family (1) Charles Robert Darwin; (2) Francis Galton; and (3) Ralph Vaughan Williams.

  5. students should learn the concept of productive struggle

    6. Productive struggle refers to the process of effortful learning that helps students develops grit and creative problem solving. When students face problems they don’t immediately know how to solve a problem, we don't want them to give up.

This project provided many problems which students had to overcome. I hope it has increased their “grittiness”, and creative problem solving skills.
